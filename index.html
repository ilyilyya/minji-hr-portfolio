GROWTH PROCESS

"If you know how to love yourself, then love and care for others."

This has been the guiding value in my life. I believe that one’s attitude is the mirror of the heart and reflects one’s thoughts. Moreover, I believe that such an attitude is shaped through everyday actions. When you are honest with yourself and compassionate towards others, accepting them for who they are, you often find that people naturally develop a positive impression of you—even love you. I experienced this firsthand during my school years, realizing that a single kind word could dramatically affect someone’s day.

In the past, I was highly critical of myself. Constantly feeling that I was academically behind or lacking compared to others, I fell into the habit of comparing myself to everyone, which led me to dwell in negative emotions such as sadness, self-pity, despair, and a sense of failure. This self-deprecating mindset adversely affected the way I interacted with others and strained my relationships. Eventually, I recognized that if I viewed myself negatively, I would end up viewing others through a similarly distorted lens. I came to understand that respecting and loving oneself is the foundation for respecting others. After learning to love myself and change my attitude, I noticed that those around me began to see me in a better light. I realized that my improved attitude was not only a reflection of my own perspective on life but also a decisive factor in how I felt about and chose those around me. This change in perspective ultimately transformed my life.

ACADEMIC JOURNEY

I have always been someone who does not fear new challenges but rather enjoys them, investing immense energy into trying new things. One of the most transformative experiences in my life was studying abroad. Feeling stifled by the rigid, grade-focused environment of high school in Korea, I chose to pursue an international education when I felt that my abilities and talents were unfairly confined. While attending a two-year college, I simultaneously completed my high school education. A decision born out of the unbridled confidence of my younger self. This experience reaffirmed my strong belief in myself, even though it came with significant responsibility and pressure. I learned that self-confidence can generate tremendous synergy in achieving goals.

Additionally, I played a supportive role at the International Office by helping students who struggled with English. I was actively involved in planning orientation events for new students every semester and organizing various school events, which encouraged and motivated the students. Through these activities, I learned that effective communication isn’t merely about listening; it involves understanding the underlying motives and emotions behind what someone is saying, as well as providing thoughtful feedback. These experiences taught me that people feel comforted and trusted when they have someone who truly listens to their stories. Every conversation I had reinforced the importance of attentive listening, ultimately reshaping my communication style and approach to dialogue. These experiences also helped me grow personally, as I was able to improve upon my shortcomings through interactions with a diverse group of students.

STRENGTH & WEAKNESS

I consider my adaptability to adjust quickly to changing environments to be one of my greatest strengths. During the five years I studied in the United States, whenever I encountered communication challenges or felt my own limitations, I refused to give up and instead committed to learning and improving through constant effort and interaction with many people. As a result, I was able to communicate effectively with friends from diverse backgrounds, overcoming language barriers in the process. Although my English was not perfect at first, working on personal assignments with American friends and engaging in casual conversations helped me acquire new expressions, idioms, and a more natural way of speaking. Moreover, I often acted as an intermediary during team projects by sharing my insights with Korean students, which further honed my communication skills. I also assisted newly arrived international students in adapting to the school environment, ensuring they felt a sense of belonging. Even when these students were not yet fully proficient in English, I communicated with them through eye contact, gestures, and words to understand and empathize with their challenges. I believe that my approach to communication and my willingness to embrace change have made me a competent and genuine communicator.

On the other hand, my eagerness to achieve results has sometimes been a double-edged sword. During my college years, I balanced part-time jobs, club activities, and collaborative projects simultaneously. In my attempt to juggle everything, I once nearly missed a project deadline, which could have adversely affected my teammates. Focusing intensely on one project sometimes led me to neglect my responsibilities in other areas, leaving my club members feeling that I had not fulfilled my duties as a team member. This experience taught me to temper my ambition and to plan more realistically based on my abilities and available time. I have since worked on prioritizing and managing my commitments more effectively.
